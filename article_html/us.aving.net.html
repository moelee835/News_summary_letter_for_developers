DESIGNOVEL presents AI search visibility platform ‘BVI’ at MWC 2026… “AEO and GEO strategies are essential beyond SEO in the AI era.”

* AI startup from POSTECH Knowledge and Language Engineering Lab unveils AEO/GEO-based brand visibility analysis solution.
* Diagnose brand exposure status within generative AIs like ChatGPT and Gemini by simply entering a URL or product name
* NVIDIA Inception Program member continuing multi-year GPU support and joint research collaboration
* Presents marketing strategies for the AI era, targeting global expanding brands and B2B technology companies

AI-based brand visibility analysis startup DESIGNOVEL (CEO Ki-Young Shin) participates in ‘MWC (Mobile World Congress) 2026,’ held in Barcelona, Spain, from Monday, March 2 to Thursday, March 5 (local time).

DESIGNOVEL appeared at ‘4YFN (4 Years From Now),’ the startup exhibition program of MWC, and showcased its AI search optimization platform BVI (Brand Visibility Index) centered on AEO (AI Engine Optimization) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) solutions.

DESIGNOVEL was founded by CEO Ki-Young Shin, who hails from the Knowledge and Language Engineering Laboratory at POSTECH. “People these days don’t search; they ask AI,” Shin explained regarding the background of the company’s founding. “Our company, and the products or information we consider important, must be exposed within those answers, but there are almost no companies that can manage or solve this.” His core concern is that, while people’s information exploration methods are rapidly shifting from Google keyword searches to generative AI conversations like ChatGPT and Gemini, most companies do not even understand how their brands are described in these conversations.

The BVI platform, which DESIGNOVEL focused on demonstrating at this exhibition, comprehensively analyzes how a brand is exposed in major generative AIs such as ChatGPT and Gemini when a URL or product name is entered. In a live on-site demo, detailed indicators were calculated in real-time for MWC Barcelona, showing an AI visibility score of 83.4 (out of 100), brand authority of 92, and historical influence of 90.

“Once the analysis results are out, it finds the reasons behind those scores and shows which items are doing well or poorly compared to competitors,” Shin explained. “AI agents then fix the problems over three months, six months, or a year based on the derived action items.” The core differentiator of BVI is this end-to-end structure, where the AI directly performs improvement work beyond simple diagnosis.

DESIGNOVEL has continued collaboration for several years as a startup member of the NVIDIA Inception Program. The company has received support from NVIDIA in various fields, including GPU hardware support, joint research and development, and invitations to global forums. At the MWC site, an NVIDIA representative was captured visiting the DESIGNOVEL booth to deliver the Inception Program member badge.

At this MWC, DESIGNOVEL targeted two main groups. The first is brands seeking to expand overseas from Korea. CEO Shin advised, “Try searching for your brand on ChatGPT in the global market,” emphasizing that AEO and GEO strategies are essential beyond traditional SEO (Search Engine Optimization) to build global brand awareness in the generative AI era.

The second group is global B2B technology companies participating in MWC. “When I asked the numerous companies participating in the exhibition if they knew how their technology was being described in AI, most did not know,” he stated. “I came here to help ensure that evaluations such as ‘trustworthy’ or ‘high performance’ are naturally exposed in AI answers.”

At a time when the search paradigm is shifting from keywords to conversations, his argument is that designing how AI ‘speaks’ about a brand is the essence of next-generation marketing.

MWC (Mobile World Congress) 2026, hosted by the GSMA (Global System for Mobile Communications Association), is held at the Fira Gran Via exhibition center in Barcelona, Spain, under the theme ‘The IQ Era’. This year’s event is the largest ever, with approximately 2,900 companies from 205 countries and more than 100,000 visitors participating. Major exhibition fields encompass the entire ICT industry, including AI infrastructure, 5G Advanced, and 6G preliminary technology, mobility, and fintech. Additionally, more than 200 conference sessions involving global leaders run in parallel during the exhibition period.
