[MWC 2026] Langcode presents enterprise AI agent platform ‘CXP Series’ at MWC 2026… “The era where agents complete entire enterprise tasks”

* Unveils 3-tier solution lineup: CXP Agent, CXP Workflow, and CXP Hub
* Equipped with proprietary core technologies, including Advanced RAG, NL2W, and Orchestration Engine
* Secures global references, including Mercedes-Benz and Dubai government agencies

SNS 기사보내기

I share this article

Enterprise AI agent platform startup Langcode Inc. (CEO Minjun Kim) participates in ‘MWC (Mobile World Congress) 2026,’ held in Barcelona, Spain, from Monday, March 2 to Thursday, March 4 (local time), showcasing three solutions from its CXP series.

Founded in 2020 by CEO Minjun Kim, a graduate of Seoul National University, Langcode operates a ‘3C (CXP, Consulting, Cloud)’ business model that combines software development consulting and cloud solutions centered on its conversational AI Agent platform. The company has attracted approximately 4 billion KRW in cumulative investment. It boasts a roster of large domestic and international enterprise clients, including Mercedes-Benz, Samsung Heavy Industries, 7-Eleven, KT, and LS ELECTRIC.

The CXP series presented at this exhibition consists of three solutions: CXP Agent, CXP Workflow, and CXP Hub. “Competitiveness in the enterprise AI agent market is ultimately determined by two pillars: performance and function,” an official stated in an interview. “The core is whether it provides appropriate answers to user questions and whether it perceives the context of a work request to carry it through to completion.”

The first product, CXP Agent, generates answers from internal corporate data. By linking various source data—such as Office documents, note-taking platforms like Notion, and emails—it derives optimal answers through a combination of Advanced RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation), Re-rank technology, and optimized chunking strategies. A key feature is that it provides the sources of the documents used to formulate the answers.

The second product, CXP Workflow, covers a much broader scope of work than CXP Agent. Its NL2W (Natural Language to Workflow) engine decomposes a user’s natural language request into work units. It completes them end-to-end, from data source collection and processing to generation and execution. For example, Shinhan Bank uses APIs to pull corporate information disclosed on DART, combines it with internal analytical data, and automatically performs ROE evaluations and business-insight analyses to produce complete reports. The essence of CXP Workflow is that the AI designs and executes the entire complex business process beyond knowledge-based Q&A.

The third product, CXP Hub, is a platform that integrates various agents—whether built internally or through external solutions—into a single place. Even if a user does not know exactly which agent handles a task, the orchestrator engine decomposes the request at the Hub, automatically routes it to the appropriate agent, and coordinates execution reliably. It supports major standards such as MCP and A2A and can flexibly connect internal and external AI agents in non-standard environments through its own moderator technology. While CXP Workflow is a multi-agent system running within Langcode’s own solution, from planning to execution, CXP Hub is a higher-layer component that integrates with heterogeneous agents already owned by the company.

The company’s performance in overseas markets is also notable. Langcode has supplied solutions to global companies, including Mercedes-Benz and Chanel APAC. Last year, the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) in Dubai introduced CXP Hub and achieved excellent results in a PoC (Proof of Concept) operating six agents. Recently, the company has been discussing agent hub-based projects with ST Engineering in Southeast Asia.

Langcode’s primary goal at MWC is to discover new customers in the European market, targeting the financial sector, which is sensitive to data privacy, and the manufacturing sector, which requires vertical (industry-specific) specialization. The solutions are designed to comply with global regulations such as the EU AI Act and GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation), and they feature scalability from the outset to enable agents to perform everything from general tasks to industry-specific operations.

MWC (Mobile World Congress) 2026, hosted by the GSMA (Global System for Mobile Communications Association), is held at the Fira Gran Via exhibition center in Barcelona, Spain, under the theme ‘The IQ Era’. This year’s event is the largest ever, with approximately 2,900 companies from 205 countries and more than 100,000 visitors participating. Major exhibition fields encompass the entire ICT industry, including AI infrastructure, 5G Advanced, and 6G preliminary technology, mobility, and fintech. Additionally, more than 200 conference sessions involving global leaders run in parallel during the exhibition period.
